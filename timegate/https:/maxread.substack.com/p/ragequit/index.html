Okay, but, what is a DAO, you ask? The basic idea is that, through the use of tokens on the blockchain, people can create leaderless, self-directed organizations. The tokens essentially act as shares; they give you the ability to make and vote on proposals (like how the group can spend its pooled funds, for example), and you can earn them for doing work for the group ‚Äî programming, serving on committees, providing liquidity, etc.

Importantly, in most cases you can buy or sell DAO tokens on the open crypto market. See a cool DAO you want to join? Buy the tokens. Disagree with the last vote your DAO took? The idea, as far as I can understand it, is to align the incentives of group members by attaching the financial value of their token holdings to the overall health and value of the group. The more people want in to your network, the more valuable your tokens, so you‚Äôll want to make a group that people want to join.

The most famous and influential deployment of the idea of ‚Äúexit‚Äù is Albert Hirschman‚Äôs 1970 book Exit, Voice, and Loyalty. In the context of the 2021 internet, dominated by inescapable platforms over which you have no control2, you can understand the appeal of organizations built around the ragequit. DAOs takes the scaled-up, Thiel-Musk-Bezos-Friedman-Land vision of the civilizational ragequit and scales it back down to the group.
